Propensity score methods in causal inference

Propensity score (PS) weighting is a famous method in causal inference, which is useful in mitigating the imbalance of covariates in observational data. A current research focus of mine is methods for addressing the violation of positivity assumption in PS weighting. I started to work in this topic with Dr. Roland Matsouaka when I was a first-year Master student at Duke Biostatistics. Now, we have some research results about the relationship among the average treatment effect (ATE), the average treatment effect on treated (ATT), on control (ATC) and on overlap population (ATO, ATB, ATM, ATEN etc.). We have invesigated their relationship under different proportion of treated subjects (exposure), their variance estimation methods, etc.

Please see the related working papers in my CV.

I am also exploring more interest and ideas in the field of causal inference, while trying to survive from the first-year of my PhD program and being a PhD candidate...
